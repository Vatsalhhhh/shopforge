# ShopForge — Business Roadmap

A practical, founder-first guide from zero revenue to sustainable e-commerce business.

---

## Phase 0: Foundation (Now — Month 1)

### Pick Your Niche Before Writing Another Line of Code

The #1 mistake founders make is building a general store. Winning e-commerce is niche e-commerce.

**Niche selection criteria:**
- **Passion + knowledge**: You should understand the customer deeply
- **Underserved**: Amazon sells it, but nobody does it *well* with curation/community/expertise
- **$40–$300 AOV (average order value)**: cheap products kill margins; expensive products extend sales cycles
- **Repeat purchases**: consumables, subscriptions, or replacement parts beat one-time buys
- **Non-commoditized**: the product or curation has to be differentiated

**Proven niche archetypes to evaluate:**
1. Hobbyist gear (fountain pens, mechanical keyboards, telescopes, fishing, archery)
2. Professional tools for a trade (barbershop supplies, chef equipment, ceramics)
3. Curated natural / eco products (non-toxic home, zero-waste kitchen, organic baby)
4. Pet specialty (a specific breed or lifestyle — raw feeding, anxiety relief, senior dogs)
5. Cultural / diaspora products (authentic foods, fashion, home decor from a specific origin)

**How to validate in 2 weeks (before sourcing):**
1. Post in 3 Reddit communities relevant to your niche: "What's the hardest product to find in [niche]?"
2. Set up a Typeform landing page with email capture and $10 Facebook ad targeting the niche
3. Create a free newsletter (Beehiiv) with 3 curated product posts — see who subscribes
4. Goal: 100 email subscribers before you source a single product

## Phase 1: Product Sourcing (Month 1–2)

### Finding Products

**Option A — Dropshipping (low risk, low margin, fast start)**
- AliExpress, CJ Dropshipping, Zendrop, Spocket (US/EU suppliers)
- Pros: no inventory, no upfront cost; great for validation
- Cons: 15–30 day shipping kills reviews; 10–20% margins
- Use only for initial validation; migrate to private label or wholesale after product-market fit

**Option B — Wholesale (medium risk, better margins)**
- Faire.com (US small brands), Abound, direct brand outreach via LinkedIn
- Minimum orders $200–$2000; 40–60% margins typical
- Best for: curated, premium-feeling stores with real brand story

**Option C — Private Label (high risk, highest potential)**
- Source generic product from Alibaba; add your brand, packaging, formulation tweaks
- MOQ: 200–500 units; 60–80% gross margins possible
- Best for: consumables where branding drives loyalty (skincare, supplements, candles)

### Pricing Strategy

```
COGS (landed cost including shipping, duties)   = $X
Target gross margin                              = 50–65%
Retail price                                     = COGS / (1 - target margin)

Example: $12 COGS → $30 retail = 60% gross margin
After COGS, marketing (~20%), ops (~10%): ~30% net margin
```

**Psychological pricing anchors:**
- $X9.95 beats $X0.00 below ~$50
- Above $100: round numbers ($149, $199) signal quality
- Bundle at 10–15% discount to increase AOV

## Phase 2: First 100 Customers (Month 2–4)

### The Only Channels That Work Early

Don't spread thin. Pick ONE acquisition channel and be relentless.

**Channel 1: Reddit / Niche Communities (free, high-trust)**
- Contribute genuinely for 2–3 weeks before mentioning your store
- Share expertise, answer questions, post buying guides
- Soft-link in bio or at end of detailed answer posts
- Works best for: hobbyist niches with active subreddits

**Channel 2: TikTok/Reels Organic (free, high reach potential)**
- "Product unwrapping" and "why I switched to X" formats convert
- 3–5 videos/week minimum; first 30 days is output, not quality
- Works best for: visual products, lifestyle, before/after

**Channel 3: Micro-Influencer Seeding (low cost)**
- 1K–50K follower accounts in your niche; DM with free product offer
- 10–20% will post; cost is only COGS + shipping
- Track with UTM links + discount codes
- Works best for: beauty, fitness, food, pets

**Channel 4: SEO (slow, compound, high ROI)**
- Target "best [product type] for [use case]" + "vs" + "[problem] solution" keywords
- 1 long-form product guide per week (1500+ words)
- Build links via guest posts on niche blogs
- Takes 6–12 months; worth starting now

**First 100 customer playbook:**
1. Month 2: Seed 20 micro-influencers + post 3x/week on social
2. Month 2–3: Offer 20% launch discount code to email list
3. Month 3: Run $300 test on Meta ads targeting lookalikes of your email list
4. Month 4: Double down on whatever drove 60%+ of your first 100 orders

## Phase 3: Customer Retention (Month 3+)

### Retention is profit. Acquisition is cost.

**Email automations to build immediately (revenue impact ranked):**
1. **Abandoned cart** (3-email sequence, 1h / 24h / 72h) — 5–15% recovery rate
2. **Post-purchase nurture** (thank you + usage tips + review ask at Day 3 / Day 7 / Day 21)
3. **Replenishment reminder** (for consumables: "running low?" 30–45 days post-purchase)
4. **Win-back** (lapsed 90-day customers: 15% off offer)
5. **Browse abandonment** (viewed product 2+ times, didn't buy)

**Use Klaviyo** — best-in-class for Shopify-style email. Connect via ShopForge webhook events.

**Loyalty loop:**
- Points for purchase, review, referral
- Tier up: Bronze / Silver / Gold with perks (free shipping, early access, birthday discount)
- ShopForge has a coupon system ready; wire points as backend coupons

**Review strategy:**
- Email at Day 14 asking for review (not Day 7 — product needs to be used)
- Offer 10% off next order for leaving a review with photo
- Respond to every review publicly (builds brand trust)

## Phase 4: Paid Acquisition (Month 4–6)

### Only start paid ads when you have:
- 20+ organic orders (proof the product sells)
- 3+ reviews (social proof for ads)
- Profitable unit economics (COGS + fulfillment + CAC target leaves positive contribution margin)

**Meta Ads (Facebook/Instagram) — best for consumer goods**
- Start with Advantage+ Shopping campaigns (ASC) — Meta's AI optimizes audience
- Creative is the variable: test 3 video + 3 static creatives; kill losers at $50 spend each
- Target CPA = AOV × 0.3 (spend max 30% of order value to acquire a customer)
- Scale: if ROAS > 2.5 after $500 spend, double budget weekly until CPM rises

**Google Shopping — best for products with search intent**
- Product feed via Google Merchant Center
- Start with Standard Shopping; graduate to Performance Max
- Works best when product has an established search term

**CAC benchmarks by channel (2025):**
- Organic social: $5–15
- Email/SMS: $2–8
- Influencer: $15–40
- Meta ads: $25–60
- Google Shopping: $20–50

## Phase 5: Operations & Legal (Month 1 onward)

### Business Formation
- **USA**: Form an LLC in Wyoming or Delaware (~$50/year) — liability protection, simple taxes
- **India**: Private Limited or LLP via MCA portal
- Open a business bank account before you take any revenue

### Legal Documents (required before launch)
- [ ] Terms of Service
- [ ] Privacy Policy (GDPR + CCPA compliant if targeting EU/CA)
- [ ] Return & Refund Policy (be generous early — reduces chargebacks)
- [ ] Cookie consent banner

Use Termly.io or Iubenda to generate compliant policies in minutes.

### Taxes
- **USA**: Collect sales tax in states where you have nexus (typically your home state + states with $100K revenue threshold)
- Use TaxJar or Avalara to automate — do not do this manually
- **VAT (EU)**: Use the OSS scheme once EU revenue exceeds €10,000/year

### Chargebacks & Fraud
- Stripe Radar is enabled by default — blocks most fraud
- Require CVV + AVS match on all orders
- Flag orders with: mismatched billing/shipping, prepaid cards, high-value first orders
- Chargeback rate must stay below 0.9% (Stripe threshold) — disputes cost $15 each

## Phase 6: Scale (Month 6–18)

### Unit Economics to hit before scaling:
- **Gross margin**: ≥50% (ideally 65%+)
- **LTV:CAC ratio**: ≥3:1 (customer lifetime value vs. cost to acquire)
- **Payback period**: <90 days on paid channels
- **Net margin**: ≥15% after all costs

### Growth levers in order of leverage:
1. **Increase AOV**: bundles, upsells at checkout, free shipping threshold above current AOV
2. **Increase purchase frequency**: subscription option, replenishment reminders, loyalty
3. **Reduce CAC**: better creative, referral program, SEO content compounding
4. **Expand SKUs**: add adjacent products that your existing customers already buy

### Hiring order:
1. Part-time customer support (VA, 20 hrs/week) — Month 3–4
2. Freelance content creator / social media — Month 4–6
3. Full-time operations / fulfillment — Month 6–9
4. Paid media specialist — Month 9–12 (once you have budget to manage)

### Fulfillment at scale:
- **0–50 orders/month**: self-fulfill or 3PL with no minimums (ShipBob, Fulfillrite)
- **50–200 orders/month**: 3PL with dedicated pick/pack
- **200+ orders/month**: negotiate custom rates; consider 2 warehouses for East/West coast coverage

## Phase 7: Defensibility (Month 12+)

By month 12, you need at least one moat — something competitors can't copy:

1. **Brand**: Your aesthetic, voice, community feel — hard to replicate
2. **Community**: Discord, Facebook Group, newsletter list — owned audience
3. **Private label**: Your formulation/SKU is exclusive
4. **Content/SEO**: 50+ articles ranking for niche search terms
5. **Supplier relationships**: exclusive or preferred pricing through volume
6. **Subscription base**: recurring revenue that smooths cash flow
